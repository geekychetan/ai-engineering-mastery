# Welcome — Read This First 👋

You're about to start a 15-week journey from near-beginner to **Junior AI/LLM Engineer**. Everything you need is already in this repo. Here's how to begin and how we'll work together.

## Your very first step

Read **[GETTING_STARTED.md](GETTING_STARTED.md)** top to bottom. It's self-contained and explains the whole system. Then open [`daily/week-01/day-01/README.md`](daily/week-01/day-01/README.md) and start.

## Your daily loop

1. Open the top card in **"This Week"** on the project board.
2. Open its plan: `daily/week-NN/day-NN/README.md`.
3. Do the homework **on a branch**, then open a PR whose description says `Closes #<issue-number>`.
4. **Review your own diff** on GitHub (this catches half your bugs), then merge.
5. Fill the **reflection** at the bottom of the day's README before you finish.

Move your issue card across the board as you go: **This Week → In Progress → In Review → Done**.

## Expectations

- ~5 focused hours/day, 5–6 days a week. Keep **one rest day** — it prevents burnout.
- **Ship something every day**, even if small. Momentum matters more than perfection.
- Being confused before it clicks is normal — that struggle *is* the learning. Don't skip it by copy-pasting answers you don't understand.

## When you're stuck (the blocker protocol)

Try on your own for **~30 minutes** first. If you're still blocked:

1. Comment on that day's GitHub issue with: **(a)** what you're trying to do, **(b)** what you already tried, **(c)** the exact error message.
2. Add the **`blocked`** label to the issue.
3. Push your work-in-progress branch so I can see your actual code.

Writing a clear help request is a real engineering skill — this is good practice, not a failure.

## How we'll check in

- I'll look at your merged PRs and your daily reflections **every day** and leave comments — expect feedback within ~24 hours.
- We'll do a short **weekly review** together (you'll open a `Weekly Review — Week NN` issue each week).
- If you're moving faster or slower than the plan, tell me — we'll adjust. The plan serves you, not the other way around.

## A note on the workflow

`main` is protected: you can't push to it directly, and CI (automated tests) must pass before a PR can merge. This is exactly how real engineering teams work — you're building the habit from day one.

Welcome aboard. Ship something small today. 🚀
